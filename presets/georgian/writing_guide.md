# Georgian Writing Guide

## Voice

Georgian's voice is confident and curious — conviction is earned with evidence, not asserted. Write like a practitioner who has benchmarked, shipped, and seen the tradeoffs firsthand.

### Tone Pillars

| Pillar | Write Like This | Avoid This |
|--------|----------------|------------|
| Practitioner-first | "We benchmarked three open-source LLMs for structured output reliability." | "AI is transforming everything about how companies operate." |
| Earned confidence | "Based on four customer calls and our internal RAG benchmarks, we believe..." | "This is obviously the future of the industry." |
| Plain precision | "Fine-tuning adds latency and increases infra cost by ~30% in our tests." | "Leveraging cutting-edge optimization techniques to unlock synergies." |
| Warm directness | "If you're evaluating vector DBs, here's what we'd tell our portfolio." | "It is perhaps worth considering that some practitioners might find..." |

## Post Structure

| # | Section | Guidance |
|---|---------|----------|
| 1 | Hook (1–2 sentences) | Start with a concrete observation, a data point, or a provocative claim. No scene-setting fluff. |
| 2 | Context (1 paragraph) | Why does this topic matter now? Ground it in a real trend, customer problem, or market signal. |
| 3 | Thesis | One clear sentence: what will the reader learn or take away? |
| 4 | Body Sections (2–4) | Each section has a bold sub-header. Lead with the point, then support it. Prose first, bullets sparingly. |
| 5 | Evidence & Examples | Cite data, benchmarks, customer language (anonymised), or expert calls. |
| 6 | Implications | What should the reader do differently? Make it actionable. |
| 7 | Closing | Restate the thesis. One short paragraph. No "in conclusion" preamble. |

## Length

- Deep-dive / thesis posts: 1,200–2,000 words
- Tactical how-to posts: 800–1,200 words
- Event recaps / news commentary: 400–700 words
- Do not exceed 2,000 words without a clear reason.

## Titles

- Sentence case only (not Title Case).
- Active voice, clear subject — who is doing what.
- Front-load the most compelling word or phrase.
- State the answer rather than posing a question.
- Aim for 6–10 words; never exceed 14.
- Write the title last, after the argument is clear.

**Weak:** "Exploring the Potential of AI in Drug Discovery: What Might the Future Hold?"  
**Strong:** "AI is reshaping early-stage drug discovery — here is where we see the signal"

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Cite specific data, benchmarks, or customer evidence | Make broad market claims without a source |
| Lead with the practitioner angle — what you built, tested, or observed | Open with industry overview / "AI is transforming X" framing |
| Keep paragraphs to 3–4 sentences maximum | Write walls of text without visual relief |
| Include a disclosure footnote when citing portfolio companies | Publish portfolio company logos without the IP disclaimer |
| Write the title last, after the argument is clear | Repurpose a slide title directly as the blog post title |
| Use plain English before jargon | Use em dashes |
