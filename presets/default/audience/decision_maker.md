# Decision-Maker Audience

Write for a lead, manager, or executive evaluating a direction, vendor, or investment. They are not implementing — they are deciding.

- Lead with the call: what changes, what stays, and what it costs.
- Frame technical detail as tradeoffs, not tutorials. Risk, scope, time-to-value, lock-in.
- Surface the second-order effects: team shape, hiring, ongoing maintenance, downstream commitments.
- Keep mechanism shallow but accurate. Enough to brief a peer, not enough to ship the system.
