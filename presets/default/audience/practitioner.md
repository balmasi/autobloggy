# Practitioner Audience

Write for a technical doer — engineer, ML practitioner, applied researcher — who is reading to apply something this week.

- Lead with mechanism. They want to know how it works, not why it matters in the abstract.
- Show concrete shapes: code, schemas, commands, numbers. Hand-waving loses them fast.
- Name failure modes, edge cases, and the conditions under which the approach breaks. Trust is built by acknowledging limits.
- Skip introductory definitions for terms the role already owns.
