# Default Writing Guide

## Voice

Sound like a practitioner who has shipped something and learned from it. Clear, grounded, technically credible. Let conviction come from specificity — not from hype or volume.

## Prose

- State the problem or conclusion in the first paragraph. Don't warm up.
- Explain the mechanism, tradeoff, or decision behind every claim. Assertions without mechanism are filler.
- Keep paragraphs to 3–4 sentences. One idea per paragraph.
- Use examples, consequences, or operational detail to make abstract points legible.
- Name limits and failure modes when they matter — readers trust writers who acknowledge edge cases.
- Prose before bullets. Use a list only when the items are genuinely enumerable and parallel.

## Framing

- Optimise for reader usefulness, not for marketing polish or comprehensiveness.
- Avoid slogans, taglines, and invented category language unless the source explicitly uses them.
- If the source is neutral, stay neutral. If the source is opinionated, keep the opinion tied to evidence and mechanism.
- Do not explain what the post just said. End sections when the point is made.

## Do Not

- Use em dashes.
- Slip into generic assistant phrasing ("it's worth noting", "it's important to", "in conclusion").
- Inflate certainty, capability, or impact beyond what the source supports.
- Pad with transitions that carry no information ("Now that we've covered X, let's turn to Y").
- Open with an industry overview or a definition the reader already knows.
