# Prose Patterns

## Direct Slop

Delete or rewrite:

- `in this article`, `this post will`, `we will explore`, `we will discuss`
- `it is important to note`, `it is worth mentioning`
- `no discussion would be complete without`, `in summary`, `overall`, `ultimately`
- `in today's fast-paced world`, `in a world where`, `as businesses increasingly`
- `delve into`, `dive deep into`, `navigate the complexities`, `navigate the landscape`
- `game-changing`, `cutting-edge`, `world-class`, `best-in-class`, `revolutionary`, `seamless`, `groundbreaking`
- leaked assistant scaffolding such as `as an AI language model`, `knowledge cutoff`, or `turn0search0`

## Generic Vocabulary

One vague business word may be harmless. Treat these findings as candidates and keep the term when it is precise in context.

Watch for: `leverage`, `unlock`, `streamline`, `empower`, `transform`, `optimize`, `accelerate`, `enhance`, `innovative`, `robust`, `actionable`, `synergy`, `paradigm`, `elevate`, `harness`, `foster`.

Grand metaphor words such as `tapestry`, `symphony`, `realm`, `journey`, and `testament` are only useful as detector candidates. Keep them when the article is literally about music, travel, law, religion, or another concrete domain where the word is doing real work.

## Formulaic Structures

- `not X, but Y`: state Y directly unless the contrast is the actual point.
- `not only X, but also Y`: keep it only when the two sides are a real contrast.
- `from X to Y`: replace false comprehensiveness with a concrete list.
- vague authority such as `experts agree`, `research suggests`, or `studies show`: name the source or cut the claim.
- superficial analysis tags such as `highlighting the importance of`: state what happened and why it matters in concrete terms.
- Three-part lists with equal rhythm: use two items or make the third concrete.
- Rhetorical question setup: answer directly unless the question is the reader's real question.
- Paragraph-ending aphorism: replace with the next useful implication.
- Inanimate agency: avoid making concepts perform human actions when a person, team, or system is the actor.

## Rewrite Moves

- Buzzword: name the concrete action.
- Vague claim: attach the source, metric, example, or decision it affects.
- Passive construction: name the actor.
- Over-neat contrast: state the useful side directly.
- Metronomic rhythm: split one sentence or combine two.
